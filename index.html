I’m a seventeen year old programmer, writer, and innovator based in New Delhi, India.

I’m currently a high school student at The Shri Ram School, Moulsari, and the founder of Project Saans , a social project founded to help the citizens of rural India breathe.

My goal is simple: change. I want to innovate so that I can help people across the world. I want to influence positive change.

I’ve been involved in several projects in the last few years. Currently, I am the Chief Editor of The Editorial Board at my school, and an active member of the elected Student Council. Most recently, I have been working on organizing our school's annual tech event, Megabyte. I am the acting tech head of a prominent regional science magazine, The Newston. Something that I'm really passionate about is the YouTube tutorial channel, "The OOPS Committee", that I will be launching in the next couple of weeks.

Ever since I was introduced to the world of programming, I've always ventured towards slightly unconventional areas - never tied down by a field, but always exploring and pushing the boundaries of what exists.

Today, I'm not just a web developer. I'm a cross platfrom app developer, a videographer and digital editor, an audio synchronizer, a lights and sound engineer, an animator, and of course, proficient in multiple programming languages, ranging from JS, HTML5, and CSS to JAVA, Python, and Ruby.

But I'm not just a techie. I'm also an enterprenuer, an active writer, and a budding photographer.

You can check out some of my writing work on my Medium page, and some of my photography in my portfolio.

For me, life is about expressing creativity - and these are my weapons of choice.
